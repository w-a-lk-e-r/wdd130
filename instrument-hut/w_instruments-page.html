Wind Instruments

Wind instruments use air as the medium, but the way vibrations are generated differ across different types of wind instruments.

Some use a reed that vibrates as the player blows into the instrument. Examples of reed wind instruments are saxophones and clarinets.

Other wind instruments such as trumpets and trombones, rely on vibrations from the players lips themselves. These tend to be the loudest types of instruments due to how the sounds are resonated and amplified through the instruments.

Wind Instruments cont.

Another type of wind instrument such as flutes, rely on air moving in and out to generate vibrations in the air column. This phenomenon with air movement is called flutter. Now you know where the name flute comes from!

Whatever the source, vibrations are carried through the instrument via air to produce the sounds.
